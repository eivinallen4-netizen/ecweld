What We Work With

The Right Material for the Job

Not every job calls for the same metal. We'll help you pick the right material based on the application, environment, and budget — not just what's easiest for us.

Steel

Raw structural strength. The choice for gates, fencing, structural frames, and anything that needs to hold load or take abuse. Available in various grades and profiles.

Aluminum

Lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and clean-looking. Great for decorative work, coastal environments, and anywhere you want a long-lasting finish with lower weight.

Stainless Steel

Built for the long haul. Food-safe, weather-resistant, and clean. Used for handrails, commercial kitchen work, and applications where appearance and durability both matter.

Common Questions

Custom Fabrication FAQ

What do I need to bring to get a quote? +
Anything helps — a photo, a rough sketch, a written description, or even just a verbal explanation of what you're trying to do. We've worked from napkin drawings and full engineering plans. Just start the conversation.
Can you match an existing piece? +
Usually yes. Bring a photo or the existing piece if it's portable. For decorative ironwork and custom profiles, we can replicate most designs or create something close enough to blend in.
How long does custom fabrication take? +
Depends on complexity, material availability, and current workload. Simple pieces can be turned around in a few days. Larger or more complex jobs take longer. We give you a realistic timeline during the quoting process — not an optimistic guess.
Do you do small one-off pieces? +
Yes. We don't require minimum order quantities. If you need one custom bracket, one gate, or one decorative piece, we build it. Small jobs get the same care as large ones.
Can you do both fabrication and installation? +
Yes. We can fabricate in-shop and then install on-site, or we can do the whole job in the field if that makes more sense. We'll confirm which approach fits your job during the quote.
Is there a design or consultation fee? +
No. The initial conversation and quote are free. We talk through your project, confirm the scope, and give you a price before any money changes hands.
